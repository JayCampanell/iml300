as the internet continues to pervade more and more of our lives, it has started to define design culture and language. As author Kyle Chaka writes these new coffee shops and experiences are not connected to the places where their location is, a tradition ingrained in human history. but rather they are


"authentically connected to the new network of digital geography".

As these emerge they become wildly popular, going viral turning once lowkey shops into places with 3 hour lines for pastries and matcha. But who are these for? are they for the people who are willing to wait, who pride themselves on having the most places ranked on their beli? for the people who are fluent in the tounges of new LA popups? and is that such a bad thing? an emerging class of young affluence who are in pursuit of novel experiences? a new matcha flavor, a new fusion of cultural ideas never done before. I mean in all honesty I am one of those people and this project was spawned from my uncomfortability in enjoying what my brother and I call 'gentrification culture'
